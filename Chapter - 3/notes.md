Types of altitude:
- **Geometric Altitude**  *h<sub>G</sub>* - Geometric height above sea level
- **Absolute Altitude** *h<sub>a</sub>* - Height calculated from the center/core of Earth or *h<sub>G</sub> + r* (Where r is the radius of Earth)
- **Geopotential Altitude** *h* - Altitude where height is calculated with acceleration-due-to-gravity ( *g<sub>0</sub>* ) kept constant as in sea level (Introduced later in this chapter).

**Standard Atmosphere (ISA)** — an internationally agreed upon reference model of the Earth's atmosphere (temperature, pressure, density) at various altitudes.

Layers of the atmosphere in the model are classified into two types by the gradient, *dT/dh*, or *a* as mentioned in the book.

- Isothermal Layer - Temperature constant  throughout the layer ( a = 0 )
- Gradient Layer - Temperature changes linearly throughout the layer ( a != 0)
