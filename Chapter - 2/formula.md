### Pressure with limits
Pressure at a single point can be calculated with:

$$P = \lim_{\Delta A \to 0} \frac{\Delta F}{\Delta A}$$

### Density with limits
$$\rho = \lim_{\Delta V \to 0} \frac{\Delta m}{\Delta V}$$

Where:

- ρ = density
- Δm = mass of a small volume element
- ΔV = volume element approaching zero

### Average Kinetic Energy of molecules
$$KE = \frac{3}{2}k_BT$$
Where:

- KE = average kinetic energy
- k<sub>B</sub> = Boltzmann constant
- T = absolute temperature (Kelvin)

The value of k_B is:
$$ 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \ \text{J/K}$$

---

### Equation of State for a Perfect Gas

$$p = \rho RT$$

Where:

- p = pressure
- ρ = density
- R = specific gas constant
- T = absolute temperature (Kelvin)

R is the specific gas constant which varies from one type of gas to another

---

### Specific Volume

Density ρ is the mass per unit volume. Inverse of this quantity is known as specific volume and is quite frequently used in aerospace volume. By definition,
$$v = \frac{1}{\rho}$$

Hence, from the equation of state
$$p = \frac{1}{v}RT$$

we also have $$pv = RT$$

Where:

- v = specific volume
- ρ = density
- P = pressure
- R = specific gas constant
- T = absolute temperature (Kelvin)

### Wing Loading

$$\frac{W}{S} = \text{Wing Loading}$$

Where:

- W = weight
- S = wing area

---

### Rate of Climb
$$R/C = \frac{\text{Excess Power}}{W}$$

or more formally

$$R/C = \frac{P_{available} - P_{required}}{W}$$

Where:

- R/C = rate of climb
- P_available = power available from engine
- P_required = power required for level flight
- W = weight
